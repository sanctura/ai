Summary from December 2025 framework/principle discussions.

**Remuneration Committee – Staff Increase Discussion Notes**

**1\. Context & Timing**

* **Last salary increase:** June 2024

* **Interim communication:**

  * Staff were informed in **June 2025** that salary increases would only take place in **January 2026**

  * A **bonus was paid in the interim** to bridge the gap

* This sets the expectation framework for the current discussion.

---

**2\. Scope of Review**

* Salary increases to be considered **across both regions simultaneously**:

  * **Cape Town (South Africa)**

  * **London (UK)**

* Objective is consistency in approach, while allowing for **regional economic differences**.

---

**3\. Eligibility Criteria (Who Qualifies)**

**Qualifying staff must meet all of the following:**

* Employed for **more than 6 months or 1 year?**

* Not currently:

  * On probation

  * Newly appointed

  * Under performance or role-related review

**Staff who do NOT qualify:**

* Anyone employed **less than 6 months or 1 year?**

* Anyone still in **probation**

* Anyone **under discussion** for role, performance, or structural reasons

* Anyone who has **already received a promotion or salary increase** since the last review

---

**4\. Contractual Position**

* Salary increases:

  * Are **not contractually guaranteed**

  * Are **not written into employment contracts**

  * Remain at the **sole discretion of the company**

* Increases must align with:

  * Company performance

  * Financial sustainability

  * Strategic priorities

---

**5\. Alignment with Financial Year**

* Increases should align with the **new financial year (March)** rather than arbitrary calendar dates.

* Final approval should be linked to:

  * **Company performance**

  * Financial outcomes for the prior year

  * Forward-looking affordability

---

**6\. Economic Considerations Used**

* Review conducted on:

  * CPI / inflation

  * Lending rates

  * Cost of living pressures

* Benchmarked separately for:

  * South Africa

  * United Kingdom

---

**7\. Proposed Increase Ranges**

Based on economic conditions and benchmarking:

* **South Africa:**

  * Proposed increase range: **6% – 8%**

* **London:**

  * Proposed increase range: **4% – 5%**

(Final percentage per individual may vary within these bands.)

---

**8\. Financial Impact Assessment**

* A detailed schedule will be prepared showing:

  * Staff name

  * Start date

  * Current salary

  * Eligible increase range

* This will be used to:

  * Quantify total cost impact

  * Assess affordability

  * Support final committee decision
