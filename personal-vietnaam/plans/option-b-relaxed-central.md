# Option B: The Relaxed Central - Vietnam Family Holiday 2026

## Summary

Stay closer to Da Nang for Segment A, minimizing long-distance travel and prioritizing quality over quantity. Deep cultural immersion in Hoi An's ancient streets and Hue's imperial heritage, with a buffer day before the Hyatt to ensure a smooth transition. This option trades the adventure of Phong Nha for a more relaxed pace and deeper connection to Central Vietnam's UNESCO treasures.

**Best for:** Families prioritizing ease of travel, cultural depth, and minimal logistics over adventure activities.

## Detailed Itinerary

### Segment A: June 26 - July 3 (7 nights)

---

#### Day 1: Friday, June 26 - Arrival & Ancient Town

**Location:** Hoi An
**Accommodation:** Allegro Hoi An (Little Luxury Hotel) or La Siesta Hoi An Resort & Spa
**Travel:** Flight arrives Da Nang 11:05 → Private car → Hoi An (30-40 minutes)

**Activities:**
- **11:05 AM:** Arrive Da Nang International Airport (DAD)
- **12:00 PM:** Meet private transfer
- **12:45 PM:** Arrive Hoi An, check in to hotel
- **1:30 PM:** Lunch—first taste of Central Vietnamese cuisine
- **3:00 PM - 6:00 PM:** Gentle exploration of Hoi An Ancient Town
  - Japanese Covered Bridge
  - Assembly Halls
  - Walking the lantern-lit streets
  - Adjusting to jet lag with easy pace
- **Evening:** Dinner at Morning Glory Original (safe street food in restaurant setting)
  - Watch the lanterns light up over the Thu Bon River

**Notes:** Short transfer means more time to settle in. Evening is magical in Hoi An as lanterns illuminate.

---

#### Day 2: Saturday, June 27 - The Tailoring Experience

**Location:** Hoi An
**Accommodation:** Same as Day 1

**Activities:**
- **8:00 AM:** Breakfast at hotel
- **9:00 AM - 12:00 PM:** Tailoring shop visits (critical for teen girls)
  - Visit 2-3 tailors: Bebe Tailor, Yaly Couture, A Dong Silk
  - Browse designs on Pinterest/Instagram
  - Select fabrics, get measured
  - Order custom dresses, suits, whatever they dream up
- **12:30 PM:** Lunch—Banh Mi at Madam Khanh "The Banh Mi Queen"
- **2:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** Pool time at hotel (essential in July heat)
- **5:00 PM:** Return to Ancient Town for first fitting appointment
- **Evening:** Explore night market, dinner at Mot Hoi An or local restaurant

**Notes:** Tailoring process: Day 1 measure → Day 2 first fitting → Day 3 pickup. This schedule allows for it.

---

#### Day 3: Sunday, June 28 - River & Rural Life

**Location:** Hoi An
**Accommodation:** Same as Day 1

**Activities:**
- **6:00 AM:** Optional sunrise at An Bang Beach (quiet, beautiful)
- **8:30 AM:** Breakfast
- **9:30 AM - 12:00 PM:** Tra Que Vegetable Village
  - Bicycle ride to farming village
  - Learn about organic farming, herb gardening
  - Hands-on cooking class (make spring rolls, etc.)
  - Eat what you cook for lunch
- **1:00 PM:** Return to hotel for pool/rest
- **4:00 PM - 6:30 PM:** Basket Boats at Cam Thanh
  - Round boats spun to K-Pop music
  - Crab fishing with kids
  - Golden hour light for photos
  - Embrace the absurdity—it's hilarious
- **Evening:** Final Hoi An evening, tailor pickup if ready

**Notes:** This day balances authentic (farming village) with touristy-fun (basket boats).

---

#### Day 4: Monday, June 29 - The Ocean Cloud Pass to Hue

**Location:** Hue
**Accommodation:** Azerai La Residence or Melia Vinpearl Hue
**Travel:** Private car via Hai Van Pass (2.5-3 hours)

**Activities:**
- **8:00 AM:** Breakfast, final Hoi An moments
- **9:30 AM:** Depart for Hue via Hai Van Pass
  - Stop at Lang Co Beach viewpoint
  - Summit stop: panoramic views, war bunkers to explore
  - "Top Gear" famous road—photo opportunities
- **12:30 PM:** Arrive Hue, check in to hotel
- **1:00 PM:** Lunch—Bun Bo Hue (spicy beef noodles), the city's signature
- **3:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** Perfume River cruise
  - Gentle boat ride past pagodas and tombs
  - Relaxing introduction to Hue after travel
  - Visit Thien Mu Pagoda (from the water)
- **Evening:** Dinner at garden restaurant, early night

**Notes:** The Hai Van Pass scenic route is worth the extra time—spectacular introduction to geography.

---

#### Day 5: Tuesday, June 30 - Imperial Splendor

**Location:** Hue
**Accommodation:** Same as Day 4

**Activities:**
- **7:30 AM:** Early breakfast (beat the heat)
- **8:00 AM - 11:30 AM:** Imperial City (The Citadel)
  - Forbidden Purple City—where the Emperor lived
  - Visible bullet holes from 1968 Tet Offensive
  - Electric buggies available for covering vast grounds
  - Focus tour to avoid temple fatigue
- **12:00 PM:** Lunch at local restaurant
- **1:00 PM - 4:00 PM:** Hotel pool time (critical in July heat)
- **4:30 PM - 6:30 PM:** Khai Dinh Tomb
  - The most ornate of Hue's royal tombs
  - Mix of Vietnamese and European architecture
  - Stunning mosaic work inside
  - Cooler late afternoon visit
- **Evening:** Royal cuisine dinner at Y Thao Garden

**Notes:** The siesta rule: tour 8-11:30, pool 1-4, tour again 4:30-6:30.

---

#### Day 6: Wednesday, July 1 - Ruins & Unique Hue

**Location:** Hue
**Accommodation:** Same as Day 4

**Activities:**
- **8:00 AM - 11:00 AM:** Tu Duc Tomb
  - Most poetic of the royal tombs
  - Emperor spent time here writing poetry
  - Peaceful gardens and lakes
  - Good for wandering and photos
- **11:30 AM:** Lunch
- **2:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** Thuy Tien Lake (Abandoned Water Park)
  - THE highlight for teens
  - Massive rusting dragon structure
  - Global icon of "ruin photography"
  - Edgy, unique, Instagram gold
  - Perfect contrast to traditional temples
- **5:30 PM:** Return to hotel for pool
- **Evening:** Final Hue dinner, pack for Da Nang

**Notes:** Abandoned Water Park access can vary—check current status. Small "fee" may apply.

---

#### Day 7: Thursday, July 2 - Buffer Day in Da Nang

**Location:** Da Nang
**Accommodation:** Fusion Maia Da Nang or transit hotel near Hyatt
**Travel:** Private car via Hai Van Pass (2-2.5 hours)

**Activities:**
- **9:00 AM:** Leisurely breakfast in Hue
- **10:30 AM:** Depart for Da Nang
  - Second pass over Hai Van—different light, different perspective
  - Or take the tunnel for speed if tired
- **1:00 PM:** Arrive Da Nang, check in
- **2:00 PM:** Lunch at beachfront restaurant
- **3:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** My Khe Beach time or hotel pool
- **5:30 PM:** Marble Mountains (optional)
  - Buddhist temples in marble caves
  - Elevator available for those with tired legs
  - Skip if energy is low
- **Evening:** Relaxed dinner, early night before Hyatt

**Notes:** This buffer day prevents arriving exhausted at the Hyatt. Option to skip Marble Mountains entirely.

---

### Hyatt Stay: July 3 - July 13 (10 nights)

**Location:** Hyatt Regency Da Nang Resort and Spa
**Check-in:** July 3, 3:00 PM
**Check-out:** July 13, 11:00 AM

The "vacation within the vacation." Since you've already experienced Hoi An in Segment A, you can explore different activities during the Hyatt stay.

#### Suggested Day Trip Schedule:

| Day | Date | Activity | Notes |
|-----|------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Jul 3 | Arrive & settle | Explore resort facilities |
| 2 | Jul 4 | Resort day | Pool, beach, spa |
| 3 | Jul 5 | Ba Na Hills & Golden Bridge | Early departure (6:45 AM) |
| 4 | Jul 6 | Resort day | Rest from Ba Na |
| 5 | Jul 7 | Cham Islands snorkeling | Full day trip |
| 6 | Jul 8 | Resort day | Pool, beach |
| 7 | Jul 9 | My Son Sanctuary | UNESCO Cham ruins (morning) |
| 8 | Jul 10 | Resort day | Spa treatments |
| 9 | Jul 11 | Teh Dar Show evening | Lune Center, Hoi An |
| 10 | Jul 12 | Final resort day, pack | Sunset on beach |

**Key Activities:**
- **Ba Na Hills:** Golden Bridge photos essential. Fantasy Park for 9-year-old. Go early or late.
- **Cham Islands:** July optimal for visibility. Snorkeling, swimming, seafood lunch.
- **My Son Sanctuary:** Ancient Cham temple ruins (Hindu). Go early to avoid heat.
- **Teh Dar Show:** Bamboo circus—visually stunning, no language barriers.

**Notes:** Since you've seen Hoi An already, no need to repeat. Use resort days more freely.

### Segment B: July 13 - July 19 (6 nights)

---

#### Day 8: Sunday, July 13 - Northward Migration

**Location:** Lan Ha Bay (cruise)
**Accommodation:** Stellar of the Seas or Capella Cruise (connecting suites)
**Travel:** Flight Da Nang → Hanoi (1.5 hrs) + Transfer to cruise pier (2.5-3 hrs)

**Activities:**
- **7:00 AM:** Check out Hyatt
- **9:00-10:00 AM:** Flight Da Nang (DAD) → Hanoi (HAN)
- **11:00 AM:** Meet cruise limousine transfer
- **2:00 PM:** Board cruise at Got Pier
- **Afternoon:** Cruise departs, settle into connecting suites
- **4:00 PM:** Kayaking among limestone karsts
- **Evening:** Sunset on sundeck, dinner on board

**Cruise Options:**
- **Stellar of the Seas:** Pool on deck, mini-golf, wine cellar, connecting suites
- **Capella Cruise:** Waterslide from sundeck—huge hit with kids

**Notes:** 2-night cruise mandatory for reaching pristine areas.

---

#### Day 9: Monday, July 14 - Deep Bay Exploration

**Location:** Lan Ha Bay (cruise)
**Accommodation:** Same cruise

**Activities:**
- **6:00 AM:** Tai Chi on deck (optional)
- **7:30 AM:** Breakfast
- **9:00 AM - 12:00 PM:** Viet Hai Village visit
  - Bike ride through village and jungle
  - Authentic fishing community experience
- **12:30 PM:** Lunch on board, cruise to new anchorage
- **3:00 PM:** Swimming and kayaking at secluded beach
  - Lan Ha water cleaner than Halong—swimming allowed
- **Evening:** Cooking class, dinner, squid fishing from deck

---

#### Day 10: Tuesday, July 15 - To the Inland Halong

**Location:** Ninh Binh
**Accommodation:** Emeralda Resort Ninh Binh (two pools, bicycles)
**Travel:** Cruise disembark → Private car → Ninh Binh (3.5 hours)

**Activities:**
- **6:30 AM:** Sunrise on deck
- **7:30 AM:** Breakfast, pack
- **9:30 AM:** Disembark cruise
- **10:00 AM - 1:30 PM:** Transfer to Ninh Binh
- **2:00 PM:** Check in to Emeralda Resort
- **4:00 PM - 6:00 PM:** Relax at resort, explore grounds, cycle through rice paddies
- **Evening:** Dinner at resort

---

#### Day 11: Wednesday, July 16 - Karst Landscapes

**Location:** Ninh Binh
**Accommodation:** Same as Day 10

**Activities:**
- **6:00 AM - 8:00 AM:** Mua Cave (Hang Mua)
  - 500 steps to dragon-spine viewpoint
  - THE iconic Vietnam photo
  - Must go early—heat on exposed rock dangerous by midday
- **8:30 AM:** Return for breakfast
- **10:00 AM - 1:00 PM:** Trang An Boat Tour
  - 3-hour sampan through caves and karsts
  - Route 2: Kong: Skull Island film set
  - Route 3: Longest stalactite cave
- **2:00 PM:** Late lunch
- **Afternoon:** Pool time, cycling
- **Evening:** Dinner at local restaurant

---

#### Day 12: Thursday, July 17 - Capital City

**Location:** Hanoi
**Accommodation:** Sofitel Legend Metropole or Peridot Grand
**Travel:** Private car Ninh Binh → Hanoi (1.5-2 hours)

**Activities:**
- **9:00 AM:** Breakfast at resort

- **12:30 PM:** Arrive Hanoi, check in
- **1:30 PM:** Lunch—Pho (Hanoi is THE place)

- **5:00 PM:** Hanoi Jeep Tour
  - Vintage GAZ-69 Jeeps
  - Long Bien Bridge, Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, B-52 wreckage
  - Great for all ages

---

## Pros & Cons

**Pros:**
- Less travel fatigue—shorter distances, fewer transfers
- Deep cultural immersion in Hoi An and Hue
- Buffer day ensures relaxed Hyatt arrival
- Tailoring experience gets full attention in Hoi An
- Easier logistics and pacing
- Two UNESCO World Heritage towns explored in depth

**Cons:**
- Misses Phong Nha caves entirely (major adventure highlight)
- Less variety—no cave exploration in Segment A
- May see Hoi An twice (Segment A + Hyatt proximity) unless planned carefully
- Less adventurous overall
