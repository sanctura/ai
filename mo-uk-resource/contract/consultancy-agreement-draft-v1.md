## Background

A. The Company wishes to engage the Consultant to provide consultancy services relating to healthcare operations, pharmacy, and regulatory matters.

B. The Consultant has agreed to provide such services on the terms set out in this Agreement.

---

## 1. Definitions and Interpretation

1.1 In this Agreement:

**"Commencement Date"** means 10 February 2025.

**"Confidential Information"** means all information (whether written, oral, or in any other form) relating to the business, affairs, patients, clients, suppliers, plans, operations, processes, know-how, trade secrets, or software of the Company that is not in the public domain.

**"Initial Term"** means the period of one calendar month from the Commencement Date.

**"Intellectual Property Rights"** means patents, rights to inventions, copyright and related rights, trade marks, business names, rights in designs, database rights, rights in computer software, and all other intellectual property rights, in each case whether registered or unregistered.

**"Services"** means the consultancy services described in Schedule 1.

**"Working Day"** means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in England.

---

## 2. Engagement and Term

2.1 The Company engages the Consultant, and the Consultant agrees to provide, the Services on the terms of this Agreement.

2.2 This Agreement shall commence on the Commencement Date and continue for the Initial Term.

2.3 Following the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods of one calendar month ("Renewal Terms") unless terminated in accordance with Clause 10.

2.4 The Consultant confirms that they are operating as an independent contractor and not as an employee, worker, or agent of the Company.

---

## 3. Services

3.1 The Consultant shall provide the Services as described in Schedule 1 with all due care, skill, and diligence.

3.2 The Consultant shall comply with all reasonable instructions and directions given by the Company in relation to the Services.

3.3 The Consultant shall report to **Grant Merwitz, Managing Director** or such other person as the Company may designate from time to time.

3.4 The Consultant shall keep the Company informed of progress on the Services and shall promptly notify the Company of any matters that may affect the Consultant's ability to provide the Services.

---

## 4. Time Commitment and Working Arrangements

4.1 The Consultant shall provide the Services for a minimum of **15 hours per week**, to be worked across two days per week.

4.2 The specific days on which the Consultant works shall be agreed between the Parties, with reasonable advance notice of any changes.

4.3 The Consultant's normal place of work shall be **24 Newman Street, London, W1T 1PJ**, or such other location as may be agreed between the Parties.

4.4 The Consultant may be required to work remotely or at other Company locations by mutual agreement.

4.5 The Parties acknowledge that the Consultant has other professional commitments and shall work together in good faith to accommodate reasonable scheduling requirements.

---

## 5. Fees and Payment

5.1 The Company shall pay the Consultant a fee of **£46.00 (forty-six pounds) per hour** for time spent providing the Services.

5.2 Based on the time commitment in Clause 4.1, the anticipated monthly fee is approximately **£2,760.00** (15 hours x 4.33 weeks x £46), subject to actual hours worked.

5.3 The Consultant shall submit a detailed invoice to the Company at the end of each calendar month, setting out the hours worked and Services provided.

5.4 The Company shall pay each valid invoice within **14 days** of receipt.

5.5 The Consultant is responsible for accounting for and paying all taxes, National Insurance contributions, and any other levies arising from the fees received under this Agreement.

5.6 The fees are exclusive of VAT. If VAT is applicable, the Consultant shall add VAT to invoices at the prevailing rate and provide a valid VAT invoice.

---

## 6. Expenses

6.1 The Company shall reimburse the Consultant for reasonable expenses properly and necessarily incurred in the performance of the Services, provided that:

   (a) such expenses have been approved in advance by the Company in writing; and

   (b) the Consultant provides receipts or other evidence of expenditure as reasonably required.

6.2 Travel expenses between the Consultant's home and the normal place of work are not reimbursable.

---

## 7. Status

7.1 The Consultant is engaged as an independent contractor and nothing in this Agreement shall create a relationship of employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency between the Parties.

7.2 The Consultant shall not be entitled to any employment benefits including, but not limited to, pension contributions, paid holiday, sick pay, or maternity/paternity pay.

7.3 The Consultant is not authorised to enter into any contracts or commitments on behalf of the Company without prior written approval.

7.4 The Consultant shall be responsible for providing their own equipment as required for the Services, unless otherwise agreed with the Company.

7.5 The Consultant warrants that they have the right to work in the United Kingdom and shall notify the Company immediately if their immigration status changes.

---

## 8. Confidentiality

8.1 The Consultant shall keep confidential all Confidential Information disclosed to them during the course of this engagement.

8.2 The Consultant shall not, without the prior written consent of the Company:

   (a) disclose any Confidential Information to any third party;

   (b) use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than performing the Services; or

   (c) copy or reproduce any Confidential Information except as necessary for the proper performance of the Services.

8.3 The obligations in this Clause 8 shall not apply to information that:

   (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Consultant;

   (b) was lawfully in the Consultant's possession before disclosure by the Company; or

   (c) is required to be disclosed by law or regulatory authority.

8.4 The obligations in this Clause 8 shall survive the termination of this Agreement indefinitely.

---

## 9. Intellectual Property

9.1 All Intellectual Property Rights in any materials, documents, reports, or other work product created by the Consultant in the course of providing the Services ("Work Product") shall belong to the Company.

9.2 The Consultant hereby assigns to the Company, with full title guarantee, all Intellectual Property Rights in the Work Product.

9.3 The Consultant shall, at the Company's request and expense, do all things necessary to give effect to this assignment.

9.4 The Consultant waives any moral rights in the Work Product to the fullest extent permitted by law.

---

## 10. Termination

10.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time by giving **two weeks' written notice** to the other Party.

10.2 The Company may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the Consultant:

   (a) commits a material breach of any term of this Agreement;

   (b) is guilty of gross misconduct;

   (c) breaches any confidentiality or data protection obligations;

   (d) loses the right to work in the United Kingdom; or

   (e) becomes bankrupt or enters into any arrangement with creditors.

10.3 Upon termination of this Agreement, the Consultant shall:

   (a) immediately cease providing the Services;

   (b) return to the Company all property, documents, and Confidential Information belonging to the Company;

   (c) delete all Confidential Information from personal devices and systems; and

   (d) provide a final invoice for Services rendered up to the date of termination.

10.4 Termination of this Agreement shall not affect any rights or remedies that have accrued to either Party prior to termination.

---

## 11. Data Protection

11.1 Both Parties shall comply with all applicable data protection legislation, including the UK General Data Protection Regulation and the Data Protection Act 2018.

11.2 Where the Consultant processes personal data on behalf of the Company, the Consultant shall act only on the Company's documented instructions and shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect such data.

11.3 The Company will process the Consultant's personal data in accordance with its Privacy Notice, a copy of which is available on request.

---

## 12. Conflicts of Interest

12.1 The Consultant warrants that they are not aware of any conflict of interest that would prevent them from providing the Services.

12.2 The Consultant shall notify the Company immediately if any actual or potential conflict of interest arises during the term of this Agreement.

12.3 The Parties acknowledge that the Consultant has other professional commitments, including work with NHS trusts, and this shall not constitute a conflict of interest unless it directly competes with or is detrimental to the Company's interests.

---

## 13. Policies and Compliance

13.1 When working at Company premises or with Company patients, the Consultant shall comply with all relevant Company policies and procedures, including those relating to:

   (a) clinical governance;

   (b) health and safety;

   (c) infection control;

   (d) safeguarding; and

   (e) data protection.

13.2 The Company shall make relevant policies available to the Consultant.

---

## 14. Notices

14.1 Any notice under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be validly given if:

   (a) sent by first-class post to the recipient's address as stated above (or as subsequently notified);

   (b) delivered personally; or

   (c) sent by email to the address notified by the recipient.

14.2 Notices shall be deemed received:

   (a) if sent by first-class post, 48 hours after posting;

   (b) if delivered personally, on delivery; or

   (c) if sent by email, 24 hours after sending (unless a delivery failure notice is received).

---

## 15. General

15.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes any previous agreements or understandings relating to its subject matter.

15.2 No variation of this Agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

15.3 The Consultant may not assign or subcontract any of their rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the Company.

15.4 If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

15.5 A failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right under this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of that right.

15.6 This Agreement is governed by and shall be interpreted in accordance with the laws of **England and Wales**, and both Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the English courts.

## Schedule 1: Services

The Consultant shall provide the following consultancy services:

### 1. Operational Efficiency and Clinic Operations
- Review and recommend improvements to day-to-day operational efficiency
- Advise on patient flow and patient management processes
- Support the management and onboarding of patients
- Review and improve operational systems and workflows

### 2. Pharmacy, Aseptic, and Regulatory Advisory
- Assess feasibility of laminar flow and cleanroom capabilities
- Advise on medication preparation, mixing, and licensing requirements
- Provide guidance on regulatory and compliance matters
- Support supplier engagement and management (UK and European)

### 3. Leadership and Operational Support
- Support the strengthening of UK operational leadership structures
- Conduct feasibility assessments for new initiatives
- Help define operating cadence (huddles, issue tracking, escalation)
- Support cross-functional alignment

### 4. Technology and Systems (as agreed)
- Explore opportunities for technology or AI to support operations
- Provide input on systems and data-related initiatives

The specific priorities within these areas shall be agreed between the Parties and may be adjusted by mutual agreement during the term of this Agreement.
