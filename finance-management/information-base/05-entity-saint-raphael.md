## Key Observations

1. **Accounts Receivable (R8.43M):** Extremely high - likely Inc owes this for management fees. Critical to get ageing analysis and understand collection timeline.
2. **Finance Leases (R14.16M):** Massive lease obligations for medical equipment (Hypothermia machines). These are the main driver of the depreciation expense.
3. **Medical Equipment (R18.26M cost):** By far the largest asset. Housed in Saint Raphael but used by the practice.
4. **Loan Account - JLI Inc (R647K asset):** Inc owes Saint Raphael - mirrors the intercompany relationship.
5. **Epic Simian Loan (R429K):** Related party loan - same as in Inc.
6. **Owner A Funds Introduced (R6.28M):** Significant shareholder investment to fund the entity.
7. **Cash position (R106K):** Very thin for an entity with R1.3M+ monthly payroll. One month of delayed income would create a cash crisis.
8. **Sanctura Holdings Loan (R1.42M liability):** Owed to the UK holding company.
9. **Payroll is growing:** Employee net pay ranged from R432K (Mar) to R634K (Jul), currently at R464K. Headcount fluctuations visible.
10. **VAT liability (R211K):** Despite not being VAT registered - this may be employee tax or a classification issue.
