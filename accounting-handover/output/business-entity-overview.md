# Business Entity Overview

## The Three Core Entities

Sanctura operates through three primary legal entities across two jurisdictions. Understanding how they work together is essential to reading the management accounts and making financial decisions.

---

## 1. Dr James La Porta Inc (South Africa)

**Role:** Revenue generator - the medical practice

- **What it does:** All patient revenue flows through this entity. It is the medical practice where Dr James La Porta is the sole practitioner. It collects fees for consultations, IV therapies, treatments, and medications.
- **What it pays for:** Only medical supplies and prescription medications. Nothing else.
- **VAT:** Registered - submits bi-monthly to SARS
- **Employees:** None directly (only Mma, the medical officer, via a payment mechanism). All other staff are employed by Saint Raphael.
- **Banking:** Investec operating account
- **Invoicing:** Healthbridge (patient invoicing), Xero (quoting)
- **Accounting:** Inaura handles payroll processing, PAYE, VAT submissions, financial statements, and income tax returns. Ronald does Xero bookkeeping and bank reconciliation.

**Key point:** This is the top of the money funnel. Revenue comes in here, medical costs go out, and Saint Raphael invoices it for everything else via management services fees.

---

## 2. Saint Raphael (Pty) Ltd (South Africa)

**Role:** Operational backbone - the management and services company

- **What it does:** Employs all SA staff (nurses, reception, admin). Pays all non-medical operational expenses. Manages property leases, equipment financing, and practice assets. Provides management services to both the Inc and Sanctura Holdings.
- **What it pays for:** Staff salaries, supplements, cleaning, maintenance, subscriptions, office costs, HR costs, equipment financing (TAFTS loans), property leases, and all non-medical suppliers.
- **VAT:** Not VAT registered
- **Employees:** All SA-based staff except Mma
- **Banking:** Investec operating account (same dual-control system as Inc)
- **Accounting:** Inaura handles payroll/PAYE/financials. Ronald does Xero bookkeeping, bank reconciliation, and supplier management.

**Key point:** This is where the costs sit. It recovers those costs by invoicing the Inc and Sanctura Holdings for management services. This inter-company invoicing is the mechanism that distributes money from the revenue entity to the cost entity.

### Inter-Company Invoicing

| Invoice From | Invoice To | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Saint Raphael | Dr James La Porta Inc | Management services (SA staff, operations, facilities) |
| Saint Raphael | Sanctura Holdings | Management services (cross-border services) |

These invoices are the financial bridge between entities. They must be correctly priced and documented for tax compliance.

---

## 3. Sanctura Limited (United Kingdom)

**Role:** UK clinic operations

- **What it does:** Operates the UK integrative medicine clinic. Delivers IV therapy, consultations, treatments, and related medical services to UK patients. Employs UK staff directly.
- **What it pays for:** UK staff salaries, UK medical stock (Berg, Matteus/BioSomo, Dr Weber), UK operational costs.
- **VAT:** Not registered (medical exemption)
- **Employees:** UK-based clinical and admin staff
- **Banking:** Barclays (debit account + 2 credit cards), Revolut (KYC pending)
- **Accounting:** Kinari / Capricorn Capital Services handles complete accounting, banking, PAYE, financial statements, and income tax. Ronald does Xero bookkeeping.

**Key point:** Operates largely independently from the SA entities. Kinari handles the heavy lifting on compliance. Ronald monitors via Xero. Amanda is the on-the-ground practice manager.

## How Money Flows

### South Africa
```
Patients
  │
  ▼
Dr James La Porta Inc (collects revenue)
  │
  ├──► Medical suppliers (prescriptions, medical stock)
  ├──► SARS (VAT bi-monthly, PAYE monthly - James must release)
  │
  ▼
Saint Raphael invoices Inc for management services
  │
  ├──► Staff salaries (all SA staff)
  ├──► Non-medical suppliers (supplements, cleaning, maintenance)
  ├──► Property leases & equipment financing
  └──► SARS (PAYE monthly - James must release)
```

### United Kingdom
```
Patients
  │
  ▼
Sanctura Limited (collects revenue, pays all UK costs)
  │
  ├──► UK staff salaries
  ├──► UK medical stock suppliers
  ├──► UK operational costs
  └──► HMRC (PAYE via Kinari)

Saint Raphael invoices Sanctura Holdings for management services
```

## The Separation Logic

| Expense Type | Paid By |
|---|---|
| Medical supplies & prescriptions | Dr James La Porta Inc |
| All SA staff salaries | Saint Raphael |
| All non-medical SA operations | Saint Raphael |
| All UK operations & staff | Sanctura Limited |

This structure exists for:
1. **Tax efficiency** - VAT registration only where needed (Inc), medical exemption where applicable (Sanctura Ltd)
2. **Cost allocation** - Clear separation of medical vs operational costs
3. **Risk management** - Practice revenue separated from employment liabilities
4. **Regulatory compliance** - Medical practice entity distinct from management entity
