# Unit Manager (Nursing Lead) – Oncology/Chemotherapy Unit

*Version 2 — Updated 8 March 2026 following Dr James Laporta's review*

## Role Purpose

Lead the nursing function at the Cape Town clinic, ensuring all patients are treated safely and effectively according to their clinical plans. Own the clinical standard of the nursing unit — from chemotherapy administration and IV treatments to pharmacy management and the functional direction of nursing staff. Act as the clinical backbone of the Sanctura Cape Town nursing operation, enabling physicians to focus on diagnosis and treatment decisions while the nursing team delivers seamless, high-quality care.

This role covers **all treatments delivered by the nursing unit** — chemotherapy, non-chemotherapy IV infusions, and any other clinical treatments offered by the practice. The Unit Manager is accountable for the quality, safety, documentation, and billing accuracy of every treatment the nursing team delivers.

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Clinical Leadership

* Act as the clinical lead for the nursing team, setting and maintaining the standard of patient care delivery across all treatment types.
* Ensure all patients are treated according to their specific treatment plans as directed by physicians — whether chemotherapy, IV infusions (iron, testosterone, vitamins, etc.), or other clinical treatments.
* Provide clinical oversight during treatments, monitoring patient responses and intervening where necessary.
* Lead by example — model the clinical rigour, professionalism, and patient-centred approach expected of the nursing team.
* Ensure clinical handovers between shifts or staff are thorough and complete.
* Take clinical direction from Dr Ismaeel Ebrahim on protocols, treatment standards, and medical governance.

### 2. Treatment Administration & Oversight

* Oversee the preparation and administration of **all treatments** delivered by the nursing unit, including:
  * Chemotherapy — ensure correct dosing, sequencing, drug handling, and cytotoxic safety protocols
  * Non-chemotherapy IV infusions — iron, testosterone, vitamin drips, and other IV treatments
  * Any other clinical treatments offered by the practice
* **All IV treatments must be administered under a valid prescription from a treating physician, or in accordance with an approved internal protocol documented in the CRM system.** No IV treatment may be administered without a clear, traceable prescription or protocol authorisation.
* Ensure all treatment procedures follow correct clinical protocols and safety standards.
* Ensure all chemotherapy SOPs are completed and followed where relevant (refer to SOPs on Drive).
* Ensure clear protocol and script writing procedures are in place at Sanctura — all protocols must have corresponding scripts that are documented, current, and carried out as prescribed.
* Monitor patients during and after treatments for adverse reactions, intervening and escalating as required.
* Maintain up-to-date knowledge of treatment protocols, drug handling, and safe administration techniques.
* Train and mentor nursing staff on treatment administration competencies across all service lines.

### 3. Pharmacy & Stock Management

* **Own the day-to-day management of clinical pharmacy stock** — ordering, receiving, storage, stock levels, and ensuring treatment supplies are always available.
* Submit clear, well-structured stock requests to the Practice Manager for approval — requests must specify items, quantities, and clinical justification where relevant.
* **All stock orders require sign-off from the Practice Manager** before being placed — no orders go through without review and approval.
* Work with the Practice Manager to ensure stock ordering aligns with budget, supplier agreements, and procurement policy.
* **Maintain rigorous quality control records of all stock used** — every item drawn from stock must be recorded against the relevant patient treatment, ensuring full traceability from receipt to administration.
* Ensure all pharmacy storage, handling, and disposal meets regulatory and safety standards, including hazardous drug handling and cytotoxic waste.
* Coordinate with suppliers and flag any supply chain issues early.
* Between the Unit Manager and the Practice Manager, there should never be a situation where the clinic runs out of what it needs or orders what it doesn't.

### 4. Patient Billing & Clinical Documentation

* **Accountable for the accuracy and completeness of all nursing documentation**, including:
  * **Patient invoices** — ensure every treatment, medication, and consumable administered is accurately captured and the patient invoice is updated in real time. Nothing leaves the treatment room unbilled.
  * **Clinical notes** — ensure nursing clinical notes are logged for every patient interaction, recording treatments given, patient observations, and any clinical events.
* Oversee the nursing team's documentation discipline — spot-check records, address gaps, and ensure consistency across the team.
* Ensure documentation meets both clinical governance and billing requirements — accurate records protect the patient, the practice, and the team.
* Escalate billing discrepancies or system issues to the Practice Manager promptly.

### 5. Functional Management of Nursing Staff

* Provide functional direction to nursing and support staff regarding clinical tasks and daily unit operations.
* Manage staff rostering to ensure adequate coverage, continuity of care, and fair scheduling.
* Ensure nursing staff are clear on their daily responsibilities, treatment schedules, and patient allocations.
* Support the development of nursing staff through on-the-job guidance, clinical mentoring, and performance reviews.
* Address clinical performance issues directly and escalate persistent concerns to Kyle Bennett.
* Foster a culture of accountability, teamwork, and mutual respect within the unit.

### 6. Clinical Coordination & Liaison

* Work in close partnership with Dr Ismaeel Ebrahim to ensure clinical continuity between medical direction and nursing delivery.
* Maintain an open, proactive working relationship — do not wait for problems to surface before communicating.
* Collaborate with Kyle Bennett on team coverage, scheduling, stock approvals, and operational matters that affect clinical delivery.
* Participate in ward rounds and clinical reviews, providing nursing perspective and treatment updates.
* Liaise with physicians to clarify treatment directions and ensure nursing team alignment.

### 7. Compliance, Clinical Governance & Unit Readiness

* Adhere to all clinical governance, data protection, and patient confidentiality requirements under South African law.
* Ensure the nursing team operates within SANC regulatory standards and professional codes of conduct.
* Maintain safety standards across the unit — including sharps management, hazardous waste, cytotoxic handling, and emergency protocols.
* **Ensure the nursing station, nursing baskets, and resuscitation trolley are fully stocked at all times.**
* **Ensure the defibrillator and all resuscitation equipment is checked and confirmed operational on a weekly basis.**
* Maintain accurate clinical records and ensure all treatments are properly documented.
* Report clinical incidents, near-misses, and adverse events through the appropriate channels.
* Support audits and inspections by maintaining audit-ready clinical documentation.

## Success Measures

* **Clinical Standards:** All patients treated according to their clinical plans with no gaps or errors in care delivery — across chemotherapy, IV infusions, and all other treatments.
* **Treatment Safety:** All treatments administered correctly per protocol; no avoidable adverse incidents. All IV treatments traceable to a valid prescription or approved protocol.
* **Protocol Compliance:** All chemotherapy SOPs completed; all protocols have corresponding scripts that are documented and current.
* **Pharmacy Management:** No stock shortages or delays affecting patient treatments; clear stock requests submitted to Practice Manager; quality control records maintained with full traceability of stock used.
* **Billing Accuracy:** Every treatment and consumable accurately invoiced; no unbilled treatments; invoice updates completed same day.
* **Clinical Documentation:** Nursing clinical notes logged for every patient interaction; records audit-ready at all times.
* **Team Performance:** Nursing staff are clear on responsibilities, clinically competent, and delivering to standard.
* **Clinical Coordination:** Seamless liaison with Dr Ismaeel Ebrahim — no dropped handoffs between medical direction and treatment delivery.
* **Unit Readiness:** Nursing station, baskets, and resuscitation trolley stocked; defibrillator and resuscitation equipment checked weekly.
* **Communication:** Line manager (Kyle Bennett) is kept informed with no surprises; flash reports submitted on time.
* **Compliance:** Clinical documentation is audit-ready; no governance breaches.
