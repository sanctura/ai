## What the Role Requires

### 1. Operational Leadership
- Oversee the full day-to-day running of the Cape Town clinic — operational readiness, punctuality, and service excellence
- Lead morning briefings, coordinate staffing and room allocation, ensure on-time clinic start and flow
- Monitor and maintain clinic presentation — cleanliness, ambience, signage, and hospitality elements
- Step in directly to resolve operational gaps or service issues; you are both leader and operator
- Enforce strict discipline and adherence to operational routines

### 2. Systems, Processes & Performance
- Own all standard operating procedures (SOPs) — ensure they are current, locally compliant, and effectively implemented
- Use data dashboards and daily checklists to monitor performance, identify inefficiencies, and drive continuous improvement
- Maintain a culture of discipline and consistency through daily routines and audits
- Partner with leadership to design, test, and refine operational systems

### 3. Compliance & Governance (South African Context)
- Ensure the clinic meets all applicable South African regulatory and legislative standards:
  - **POPI Act** — safeguard patient and staff data in all processes and systems
  - **Health and Safety Regulations** — maintain safe working conditions, risk registers, and regular inspections
  - **Fire Safety Requirements** — oversee fire drills, equipment checks, and emergency readiness
  - **Basic Conditions of Employment Act (BCEA)** — ensure fair scheduling, working hours, and record-keeping compliance
  - **Occupational Health and Safety Act (OHSA)** — conduct hazard assessments and ensure staff awareness training
- Maintain proper documentation, logs, and reporting for inspections or internal audits

### 4. People & Culture
- Lead the front-of-house, administrative, and support staff — foster a culture of professionalism, empathy, and accountability
- Recruit, onboard, and develop team members in collaboration with HR and leadership
- Conduct performance reviews, coach individuals, and ensure the team is equipped to deliver high standards
- Ensure alignment with HR standards, policies, and disciplinary frameworks
- Manage the relationship between your team and the clinical/nursing staff — you are responsible for ensuring the non-clinical side runs so well that clinical staff can focus entirely on patient care

### 5. Financial & Administrative Management
- Manage clinic budgets, operating costs, and petty cash in line with company policy
- Approve procurement within delegated limits and maintain vendor relationships
- Monitor supplier performance and ensure timely payments, inventory accuracy, and cost efficiency
- Support finance with payroll submissions, attendance tracking, and monthly reporting (Xero)
- Manage billing accuracy — ensure invoices, quotations, and product/service pricing are correct in Medprax and Xero

### 6. Patient Experience & Service Delivery
- Ensure every patient receives a seamless, caring, and premium experience from entry to exit
- Oversee reception, appointment scheduling, and coordination between clinical and administrative teams
- Respond to feedback and resolve patient concerns promptly and professionally
- Maintain confidentiality and data integrity in all patient interactions in line with POPI standards

### 7. Facilities, Equipment & Safety
- Oversee the maintenance and readiness of all clinic spaces, equipment, and consumables
- Conduct daily readiness and safety checks — report faults or hazards immediately
- Manage relationships with building management (Ideas Cartel), cleaning services, and maintenance providers
- Ensure all fire safety systems (extinguishers, signage, evacuation plans) are in place and functional
- Maintain a safe, calm, and aesthetically consistent environment that reflects the brand's quality

### 8. Data, Reporting & Continuous Improvement
- Maintain accurate operational data (appointments, incidents, checklists, inventory)
- Generate and analyse weekly performance reports for leadership review
- Use insights to recommend and implement process improvements
- Contribute to group alignment by sharing insights and adopting best practices from the broader Sanctura network

### 9. Strategic & Cross-Functional Collaboration
- Partner with leadership to align the Cape Town clinic's performance with group strategy and growth objectives
- Support rollout of new systems, service lines, or technologies locally
- Collaborate with marketing, HR, and finance to ensure integrated execution
- Work with the Manco communication platform to ensure coordination with Dr Ismaeel and the nursing team

## Nursing Unit Oversight (New)

With immediate effect, your role expands to include administrative line management of the Cape Town nursing unit. This is a significant addition to your responsibilities and reflects the trust placed in your leadership.

### 10. Nursing Team — Administrative Line Management
- Abongile (Lead Nurse) now reports to you for all administrative and line management matters — leave, scheduling, HR documentation, performance management, and day-to-day accountability
- You are responsible for coaching and guiding Abongile in his role as Lead Nurse, setting clear expectations, and supporting his development as a clinical leader
- The nursing team (Ferdinand Cho Muluh, Candice Henri, Carel Louw) reports to Abongile clinically — you oversee the unit administratively through Abongile, not around him
- Conduct regular check-ins with Abongile to stay across nursing team dynamics, workload, and any emerging issues
- Address people matters (conduct, attendance, performance, grievances) within the nursing unit using the same standards and frameworks as your existing team

### 11. Clinical Collaboration with Dr Ismaeel Ebrahim
- Dr Ismaeel Ebrahim provides clinical guidance and direction to Abongile and the nursing team — clinical protocols, treatment plans, and clinical standards are Dr Ismaeel's domain
- You do not direct clinical care. Your role is to ensure the administrative and operational environment supports excellent clinical delivery
- Where administrative decisions affect clinical operations (e.g., staffing levels, scheduling, equipment), coordinate with both Abongile and Dr Ismaeel before acting
- Escalate any clinical concerns raised by the nursing team to Dr Ismaeel — do not attempt to resolve clinical matters independently

### 12. Pharmacy & Stock Oversight
- Abongile is responsible for managing the pharmacy — ordering clinical stock, maintaining pharmacy standards, and ensuring treatment supplies are available
- You are responsible for overseeing and signing off all stock orders in collaboration with Abongile — no orders go through without your review and approval
- Ensure stock ordering aligns with budget, supplier agreements, and procurement policy
- Work with Abongile to maintain accurate stock records in Xero and flag discrepancies or waste
- Between the two of you, there should never be a situation where the clinic runs out of what it needs or orders what it doesn't

### What This Means in Practice

**Your relationship with Abongile is the key to this working.** You are not taking over the nursing unit clinically — you are providing the structure, accountability, and administrative support that allows Abongile and his team to focus on patient care. Think of it as the same model you already run with reception and admin, extended to nursing.

**The split is clear:**
- **Kyle** → Administrative line management, people management, coaching Abongile, stock sign-off, operational coordination
- **Abongile** → Clinical leadership of nursing team, pharmacy management, day-to-day clinical decisions
- **Dr Ismaeel** → Clinical guidance, treatment protocols, clinical standards

**If in doubt about whether something is administrative or clinical — ask. It's better to check than to overstep.**

## General Expectations

### 1. Attitude
Show up positive. Be a voice of reason. Your energy affects the whole team — negativity is contagious, but so is optimism.

### 2. Communication
Keep me informed. No surprises. Share bad news early — I'd rather know about a problem when it's small than discover it when it's a crisis.

### 3. Accountability
Own your outcomes — both successes and failures. Do what you say you'll do, by when you said you'd do it. If circumstances change, tell me before deadlines slip. When something goes wrong, bring me the problem with your proposed solution.

### 4. Continuous Improvement
Look for ways to make things better. Question "the way we've always done it." Be curious, seek feedback, stay open to learning.

### 5. Strategic Alignment
Understand how your work connects to business priorities. If you're unsure whether something aligns, ask. We succeed when everyone pulls in the same direction.

## What This Means Day-to-Day

**Every morning:**
- Arrive and do a full floor walk before the clinic opens
- Run through the daily checklist — every room, every detail
- Brief the team on the day ahead

**Throughout the day:**
- Walk the floor — be visible, be the calm presence people anchor to
- Spot things that are out of place, log them, fix them or delegate them
- Be the first to notice when something isn't right — don't wait to be told
- Coordinate between reception, admin, and clinical teams so the clinic flows

**Weekly:**
- Submit flash report (Friday by 4pm) — accomplishments, challenges, next week's focus, asks
- Review and update the work board (Asana) — all tasks tracked, nothing forgotten
- Prep agenda items for our Monday 1:1

**The standard we're aiming for:**
- Every room should be ready for a VIP patient at any moment
- Every piece of equipment should be in its right place, working, and clean
- Every task assigned should be completed or escalated — never forgotten
- Every patient should leave feeling they received premium, compassionate care
- The clinic should run so smoothly that if you're not there for a day, the team knows exactly what to do
