## Communication Protocol

### "Without Prejudice" (WP) Usage by Scenario

In South African law, WP communications made during genuine settlement negotiations are inadmissible as evidence at the CCMA or Labour Court. This protection is grounded in common law privilege and recognised under the LRA. It applies **only** when you are making or exploring a settlement/separation offer — not in regular performance or management conversations.

| Scenario | WP Usage | Guidance |
|----------|----------|----------|
| 01 – Redundancy | Partial | Initial consultation letter is **not** WP. Any written settlement offer made within the consultation process **must** be labelled WP. |
| 02 – Performance | Partial | PIP/disciplinary process is **not** WP. Any settlement proposal made during that process should be clearly labelled WP. |
| 03 – Mutual (with gratuity) | Yes — from the outset | You are initiating a settlement offer. Invoke WP explicitly at the start of the conversation. |
| 04 – Mutual (no gratuity) | Yes — from the outset | Same as Scenario 03. Invoke WP explicitly at the start of the conversation. |

### How to Invoke WP Correctly

**Verbally** (at the start of any Scenario 03/04 conversation):
> *"Before we begin, I want to flag that this conversation is Without Prejudice — it is made in the context of exploring a mutual resolution and cannot be used in any formal proceedings."*

**In writing** (for any written settlement offer across all scenarios):
> Label the document or email **"WITHOUT PREJUDICE"** in bold at the top, before any substantive content.

**Do not:**
- Use WP as a blanket header on all communications — overuse dilutes its effectiveness and can imply you have abandoned a legitimate process.
- Share WP communications with third parties outside the negotiation (e.g., other staff) — this waives the privilege.
- Have an exploratory or "testing the waters" conversation without invoking WP — anything said without WP protection can be used as evidence of constructive dismissal at the CCMA.

**Rule of thumb**: Invoke WP at the start, or don't have the conversation at all.
