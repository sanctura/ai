# Head Nurse (Clinical Lead)

## Role Purpose

Lead the nursing function at the London clinic, ensuring all patients are treated safely and effectively according to their clinical plans. Own the clinical standard of the nursing unit — from prescribing and pharmacy management to the functional direction of nursing staff. Act as the clinical backbone of the Sanctura London operation, enabling physicians to focus on diagnosis and treatment decisions while the nursing team delivers seamless, high-quality care.

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Clinical Leadership

* Act as the clinical lead for the nursing team, setting and maintaining the standard of patient care delivery.
* Ensure all patients are treated according to their specific treatment plans as directed by physicians.
* Provide clinical oversight during treatments, monitoring patient responses and intervening where necessary.
* Lead by example — model the clinical rigour, professionalism, and patient-centred approach expected of the nursing team.
* Ensure clinical handovers between shifts or staff are thorough and complete.

### 2. Prescribing & Medication Management

* Prescribe medications within your prescribing domain and regulated authority, including but not limited to testosterone and iron infusions.
* Ensure all prescribing decisions are documented accurately and in accordance with clinical governance requirements.
* Stay current on prescribing guidelines and regulatory changes relevant to your scope of practice.
* Escalate any prescribing queries or edge cases to the treating physician promptly.

### 3. Pharmacy & Stock Management

* Manage and order all pharmacy stock required for patient treatments.
* Maintain accurate stock records — ensure no gaps, delays, or shortages that could affect patient care.
* Monitor stock usage patterns and adjust ordering to prevent waste and ensure availability.
* Ensure all pharmacy storage, handling, and disposal meets regulatory and safety standards.
* Coordinate with suppliers and flag any supply chain issues early to the Practice Manager.

### 4. Functional Management of Nursing Staff

* Provide functional direction to administrative and IV nurses regarding clinical tasks and daily clinic operations.
* Ensure nursing staff are clear on their daily responsibilities, treatment schedules, and patient allocations.
* Support the development of nursing staff through on-the-job guidance and clinical mentoring.
* Address clinical performance issues directly and escalate persistent concerns to the Practice Manager.
* Ensure adequate clinical coverage is maintained — liaise with Amanda Ross when staff require leave.

### 5. Clinical Coordination & Liaison

* Work in close partnership with Maria Vega (Lead Cancer Nurse) to ensure clinical continuity across patient case management and treatment delivery.
* Maintain an open, proactive working relationship — do not wait for problems to surface before communicating.
* Collaborate with Amanda Ross on team coverage, scheduling, and operational matters that affect clinical delivery.
* Participate in ward rounds and clinical reviews, providing nursing perspective and treatment updates.
* Liaise with physicians to clarify treatment directions and ensure nursing team alignment.

### 6. Compliance & Clinical Governance (Aligned with the CQC)

* Adhere to all clinical governance, data protection, and patient confidentiality requirements.
* Ensure the nursing team operates within regulatory standards and professional codes of conduct.
* Maintain accurate clinical records and ensure all treatments are properly documented.
* Report clinical incidents, near-misses, and adverse events through the appropriate channels.
* Support audits and inspections by maintaining audit-ready clinical documentation.

## General Expectations

In addition to role-specific responsibilities, the following expectations apply:

1. **Attitude** — Maintain a positive outlook and be a voice of reason. How you show up affects your team and colleagues. Positive mindset grows strong teams.

2. **Communication** — Keep your line manager informed of important happenings and information pertinent to the role and the business. No surprises. Share bad news early.

3. **Accountability** — Own your outcomes, both successes and failures. Do what you say you'll do, by when you said you'd do it. If circumstances change, communicate before deadlines slip.

4. **Continuous Improvement** — Look for ways to make things better. Question "the way we've always done it." Be curious, seek feedback, and stay open to learning.

5. **Strategic Alignment** — Understand how your work connects to business priorities. Make decisions that support our direction. If unsure, ask.

## Success Measures

* **Clinical Standards:** All patients treated according to their clinical plans with no gaps or errors in care delivery.
* **Prescribing Accuracy:** Medications prescribed within scope, documented correctly, and aligned with clinical governance.
* **Pharmacy Management:** No stock shortages or delays affecting patient treatments; accurate records maintained.
* **Team Performance:** Nursing staff are clear on responsibilities, clinically competent, and delivering to standard.
* **Clinical Coordination:** Seamless liaison with Maria Vega — no dropped handoffs between case management and treatment delivery.
* **Communication:** Line manager (Amanda Ross) is kept informed with no surprises; flash reports submitted on time.
* **Compliance:** Clinical documentation is audit-ready; no governance breaches.
