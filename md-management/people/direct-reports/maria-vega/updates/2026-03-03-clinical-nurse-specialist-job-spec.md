# Lead Cancer Nurse

## Role Purpose

Serve as the primary clinical link between physicians, the nursing team, and patients within the Sanctura oncology unit. Ensure every patient is informed, supported, and tracked through their treatment journey — managing cases with clinical precision and compassionate advocacy. Deliver consistent, high-quality coordination that enables the clinical team to operate at the highest standard.

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Patient Case Management

* Maintain and track patient treatment plans and progress records as designed by the treating physicians.
* Prepare patient cases ahead of weekly ward rounds, ensuring all documentation is current and complete.
* Coordinate clinical reviews and ensure timely follow-up on physician instructions.
* Keep accurate, up-to-date patient records in line with clinic systems and data standards.

### 2. Patient Advocacy & Communication

* Explain treatments, symptoms, and care pathways to patients to ensure they feel settled, informed, and supported.
* Serve as the primary human link between the clinical team and patients throughout the treatment journey.
* Respond to patient concerns with empathy, clarity, and professionalism.
* Escalate clinical concerns promptly to the appropriate physician or line manager.

### 3. Treatment Plan Support

* Assist in modifying treatment plans only under the instruction or guidance of a GMC-registered Doctor or NMC-registered Nurse (Head Nurse).
* Proactively clarify ambiguous or incomplete clinical directions from doctors — do not wait for problems to surface.
* Ensure treatment plan changes are accurately documented and communicated to all relevant parties.
* You do not independently prescribe medications or alter treatment plans — all such actions require direction from a GMC Doctor or NMC Nurse.

### 4. Clinical Coordination

* Maintain proactive, open liaison with the clinical team (including Lead Nurse) to align case management with clinical delivery.
* Coordinate across nursing, administrative, and medical staff to ensure seamless patient flow.
* Support ward round preparation and post-round action tracking.
* Flag operational or clinical risks early to the Practice Manager.

### 5. Compliance & Clinical Governance (Aligned with the CQC)

* Adhere to all clinical governance, data protection, and patient confidentiality requirements.
* All prescribing and treatment plan modifications must be directed by a GMC-registered Doctor or NMC-registered Nurse — never act independently on clinical changes.
* Maintain professional nursing standards and keep clinical knowledge current.
* Follow all clinic SOPs and contribute to their improvement where gaps are identified.

## General Expectations

In addition to role-specific responsibilities, the following expectations apply:

1. **Attitude** — Maintain a positive outlook and be a voice of reason. How you show up affects your team and colleagues. Positive mindset grows strong teams.

2. **Communication** — Keep your line manager informed of important happenings and information pertinent to the role and the business. No surprises. Share bad news early.

3. **Accountability** — Own your outcomes, both successes and failures. Do what you say you'll do, by when you said you'd do it. If circumstances change, communicate before deadlines slip.

4. **Continuous Improvement** — Look for ways to make things better. Question "the way we've always done it." Be curious, seek feedback, and stay open to learning.

5. **Strategic Alignment** — Understand how your work connects to business priorities. Make decisions that support our direction. If unsure, ask.
