**TERMS OF ENGAGEMENT** 

**Background**

1. The Locum represents that they possess the professional qualifications, skills, and experience necessary to provide general medical services, and that they are a self-employed General Practitioner registered with the General Medical Council (GMC).

2. The Locum is an independent self-employed contractor willing to provide the Services for the Company upon the terms of this Agreement. 

**Terms and Conditions**

3. These Terms shall govern the engagement from time to time of the Locum by the Practice as described in a Work Schedule agreed between the parties in the form appended hereto.

4. Each agreed Work Schedule shall constitute a separate engagement (‘Engagement’) and these Terms shall apply in relation to each Engagement only and there shall be no contract between the parties between Engagements.

*Engagements*

5. The Practice shall engage the Locum as a self-employed, independent contractor and not as an employee, worker, agent or partner of the Practice (and the Locum shall not present themselves or otherwise hold themselves out as being in such a capacity).

6. During an Engagement, the Locum shall, unless prevented by illness or injury, provide the Services at the Location and at the Times (and such other times as may be necessary for the provision of the Services), as described in the relevant Work Schedule (hereinafter referred to as ‘Services’, ‘Location’ and ‘Times’ respectively).

7. The Practice shall be under no obligation to offer any Engagement to the Locum, and the Locum shall be under no obligation to accept any Engagement where offered by the Practice.

8. The Locum shall notify the Practice as soon as reasonably practicable if the Locum is unable to provide the Services due to illness or injury or any other reason, or if the Locum is unable to attend at the Times agreed in the relevant Work Schedule.

9. The Fee agreed in the Work Schedule (‘Fee’) shall not be payable to the Locum pursuant to clause 25 in respect of any period during which the Services are not provided.

10. The Locum shall not be entitled to any sick pay, paid holiday or other employment benefits.

11. The Locum will be responsible for payment of the Locum’s own income tax and national insurance contributions in respect of any fees received from an Engagement.

12. If either party wishes to cancel a session agreed in a Work Schedule, they shall give reasonable prior written notice to the other party. 

***Locum obligations***

13. The Locum shall provide the Services with due care and skill to the best of the Locum’s ability.

14. The Locum shall provide the Services in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, standards, codes of practice and guidance relating to:

1) the General Medical Council;

2) the Care Quality Commission;

3) health and safety;

4) the Data Legislation; and

5) the Bribery Act 2010,

and the Locum shall promptly report to the Practice any unsafe or irregular working conditions or practices.

15. The Locum shall use reasonable endeavours to comply with the Practice’s policies and procedures as notified from time to time to the Locum (copies of which shall be provided or made available to the Locum by the Practice).

16. The Locum shall, at the Locum’s own expense, maintain:

1) registration with the General Medical Council;

2) the Locum shall provide the Practice with reasonable evidence of the Locum’s compliance on request.

17. The Locum shall promptly notify the Practice of any restrictions or conditions relating to the Locum’s fitness to practise or the matters referred to in clause 16(a).

18. The Locum shall have personal liability for, and shall indemnify the Practice against, any loss, liability, costs, damages or expenses arising from any medical or clinical negligence on the part of the Locum in connection with the provision of the Services during an Engagement.

19. The Locum shall on request promptly provide to the Practice any such information or reports as it may from time to time reasonably require in connection with the provision of the Services or the Practice.

20. The Locum may be engaged, employed or concerned in any other business, trade, profession, occupation or activity.

21. The Locum shall not use or disclose to any third party any confidential information relating to the Practice or any of its patients, save where such use or disclosure is authorised by the Practice or required by law.

22. As soon as possible after the last working day of each month, the Locum shall submit to the Practice an invoice for the Locum’s Fee in respect of the Services provided by the Locum during that month.

**Practice obligations**

23. The Practice shall maintain registration with the Care Quality Commission.

24. The Practice shall: 

1) provide reasonable support to enable the Locum to provide the Services including an orientation and any materials necessary for the provision of the Services;

2) provide personal protective equipment including, but not limited to, antibacterial cleaner, hand sanitiser, gloves, masks and other adequate personal protective equipment for face to face appointments;

3) subject to compliance with the Data Legislation, provide reasonable access to information including for the purposes of clinical care, local clinical audit, appraisal, or complaints or significant events.

25. In consideration for the provision of the Services during an Engagement, the Practice shall, within 14 days of receipt of an invoice, pay the Fee to the Locum by the Payment Method, as described in the relevant Work Schedule.

26. Except as described in the relevant Work Schedule, the Locum shall bear the Locum’s own expenses incurred in providing the Services during an Engagement.

27. The Practice shall be entitled to deduct from any sums payable to the Locum any sums that the Locum owes to the Practice at any time.

***Termination***

28. Each Engagement is a separate contract and that contract terminates at the end of the Engagement.

29. Either party may terminate an Engagement with immediate effect with no liability to make any further payment to the other party (other than in respect of any accrued Fees as at the date of termination) if it commits any serious or persistent breach of any of these Terms.

30. The Practice may terminate an Engagement with immediate effect with no liability to make any further payment to the Locum (other than in respect of any accrued Fees as at the date of termination) if the Locum:

1) is suspended or removed from the medical register of the General Medical Council;

2) ceases to hold medical indemnity cover;

3) is convicted of any criminal offence.

31. On expiry or termination of an Engagement the Locum shall return any property belonging to the Practice and any original or copy documents created or obtained by the Locum in the course of providing the Services and the Locum shall if requested by the Practice irretrievably delete any electronic information relating to the business of the Practice or any of its patients which is in the Locum’s possession or control.

***Data Protection (GDPR Compliance)***

32. Both Parties shall always comply with all applicable data protection and privacy legislation in force from time to time in the UK, including the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and any other relevant laws.

33. While providing services, the Locum may have access to or process personal data (including special category health data) relating to patients, staff, or other individuals. 

The Locum agrees to:

- process such data only as necessary for the proper performance of their duties;

- maintain strict confidentiality and security of personal data at all times;

- follow the Company’s policies, procedures, and instructions on data protection and information governance;

- not retain, copy, or disclose any personal data except as lawfully required or authorised by the Company.

34. The Locum must implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to safeguard personal data against unauthorised or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

35. The Locum must immediately notify the Company of any actual or suspected personal data breach and cooperate fully in investigating, reporting, and remediating such breaches in accordance with applicable law.

36. Upon termination of this Agreement, the Locum must promptly return to the Company, or securely destroy, any personal data in their possession, unless otherwise required by law.

***General***

37. The parties may from time to time review these Terms, provided that any variation of these Terms (or any Work Schedule agreed between the parties pursuant to these Terms) shall be made in writing and signed by both parties.

38. These Terms and any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with them shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the law of England and Wales and the courts of England and Wales shall have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with them.

39. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with these Terms shall in the first place be referred to the British Medical Association (BMA) mediation service (or such other mediation or alternative dispute resolution service as the parties may agree).
