# UK Roster Plan V1

## The Proposed UK Nursing Roster

### The Model: 3-Week Rotating Cycle

Three IV nurses (Jasmin, Maria, Ali) share three roles equally. Every 3 weeks, each nurse has done each role once.

### Shift Times

| Role | Monday | Tuesday–Thursday | Friday | Saturday |
|------|--------|-----------------|--------|----------|
| **Early** | 07:00–16:00 | 07:00–16:00 | 07:00–16:00 | — |
| **Late** | 08:00–19:00 | 11:00–19:00 | 11:00–19:00 | — |
| **Anchor** | 08:00–13:00 | 09:00–17:00 | 09:00–17:00 | 08:00–13:00 |

**What the times mean:**
- **Early nurse** (07:00 start): Opens the clinic, prepares treatment rooms for the day's protocols. Finishes at 16:00
- **Late nurse** (11:00 start, 08:00 on Monday): Covers the evening until 19:00 — last nurse out. Monday starts earlier because all staff start early on Mondays
- **Anchor nurse**: Lighter weekday hours (09:00–17:00) because they carry Saturday on-call (08:00–13:00). Short Monday (08:00–13:00) as recovery from the Saturday before. Gets a half-day Friday the *following* week as compensatory time

**Clinic coverage**: At least one nurse is present from **07:00 to 19:00** every weekday — 12 hours of coverage.

### The Rotation

| | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 | Week 4 (repeats) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Nurse A** | Anchor | Early | Late | Anchor... |
| **Nurse B** | Early | Late | Anchor | Early... |
| **Nurse C** | Late | Anchor | Early | Late... |

Every nurse gets the same deal. Nobody is permanently stuck on lates or permanently doing Saturdays.

### Saturday On-Call

- Clinic landline auto-forwards to the anchor nurse (08:00–12:00)
- If no answer: forwards to a standby nurse
- If still no answer: voicemail + SMS alert to Amanda
- After 12:00: voicemail only with a scripted message
- Nurse triages calls; escalates to doctor for red flags

### Compensatory Time

Anchor week Saturday (08:00–13:00) → half-day Friday off the following week. Simple trade.

### Coverage Support

- **Cecil (locum)**: Not part of the rotation. Available for gap-fill — leave cover, peak days, or to provide a second nurse on busy mornings
- **Maria V (specialist)**: Clinical Nurse Specialist — IV-trained but her role is different; not part of the IV nurse rotation

Amanda to assess whether single early-nurse coverage is sufficient for morning patient load, or whether Cecil should be scheduled on specific high-volume days.

### Evening Protected Time

Built into the roster: time for protocol study, documentation, and a mandatory end-of-month protocol test. Not just nice-to-have — it's structured into the schedule.

### Reception (Separate)

Two receptionists split early/late coverage. One covers Saturday phone forwarding. Goes live when the reception team is ready — doesn't need to launch with the nursing roster.

### On-Call Compensation

Deferred. Get the structure right first, then agree the compensation model separately.
