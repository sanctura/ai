## The Model: 3-Week Rotating Cycle

Three IV nurses (Jasmin, Maria, Ali) share three roles equally. Every 3 weeks, each nurse has done each role once.

## Shift Times

| Role | Monday | Tuesday–Thursday | Friday | Saturday |
|------|--------|------------------|--------|----------|
| **Early** | 08:00–17:00 | 08:00–17:00 | 08:00–17:00 | — |
| **Late** | 10:00–20:00 | 12:00–20:00 | 12:00–20:00 | — |
| **Anchor** | 08:00–13:00 | 10:00–19:00 | 10:00–19:00 | 08:00–13:00 |

**What the times mean:**
- **Early nurse** (08:00 start): Opens the clinic, prepares treatment rooms for the day's protocols. Finishes at 17:00
- **Late nurse** (12:00 start, 10:00 on Monday): Covers the evening until 20:00 — last nurse out. Monday starts earlier because all staff start early on Mondays
- **Anchor nurse**: Mid-range weekday hours (10:00–19:00) because they carry Saturday on-call (08:00–13:00). Short Monday (08:00–13:00) as recovery from the Saturday before. Gets a half-day Friday the *following* week as compensatory time

## Daily Coverage Map

| Time Slot | Nurses on Floor | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| 08:00–10:00 | 1 (Early) | Clinic opening, prep |
| 10:00–12:00 | 2 (Early + Anchor) | **Morning peak starts** |
| 12:00–17:00 | 3 (Early + Anchor + Late) | **Full peak coverage 10:00–14:00** |
| 17:00–19:00 | 2 (Anchor + Late) | Solid double cover |
| 19:00–20:00 | 1 (Late) | Final hour, wind-down |

**Monday variation**: Late starts at 10:00 instead of 12:00, so double coverage begins at 10:00 (Early + Late), then triple from 10:00–13:00 (all three until Anchor leaves).

### Peak Hour Assessment

- **10:00–14:00**: 2–3 nurses on floor throughout. Fully covered.
- **18:00–20:00**: 2 nurses (18:00–19:00), dropping to 1 nurse (19:00–20:00). For high-volume evenings, Cecil (locum) can be scheduled to provide double cover through to 20:00.

**Clinic coverage**: At least one nurse is present from **08:00 to 20:00** every weekday — 12 hours of coverage. Double or triple coverage across both peak windows.

| **Nurse A** | Anchor | Early | Late | Anchor... |
| **Nurse B** | Early | Late | Anchor | Early... |

Every nurse gets the same deal. Nobody is permanently stuck on lates or permanently doing Saturdays.

## Saturday On-Call

- Clinic landline auto-forwards to the anchor nurse (08:00–12:00)
- If no answer: forwards to a standby nurse
- If still no answer: voicemail + SMS alert to Amanda
- After 12:00: voicemail only with a scripted message
- Nurse triages calls; escalates to doctor for red flags

## Compensatory Time

Anchor week Saturday (08:00–13:00) → half-day Friday off the following week. Simple trade.

## Emergency & Backup Coverage

- **Maria Vega (Clinical Nurse Specialist)**: Available as emergency floor backup. If a nurse is called away from a patient for an emergency elsewhere in the clinic, Maria V can step in to maintain patient safety. This is **not a fixed schedule** — Maria V's primary role is as a Clinical Nurse Specialist, and she has been clear this is not her intended function. She is willing to assist on occasion when genuinely needed. This covers James's requirement for overlap/backup when there's an emergency on the floor.
- **Cecil (locum)**: Not part of the rotation. Available for:
  - Leave cover and sick days
  - Planned high-volume days (peak mornings or evenings)
  - Scheduled evening cover on busy days to ensure double nurse presence during the 18:00–20:00 peak

Amanda to assess which days/weeks typically need Cecil's support based on patient booking patterns.

## Evening Protected Time

Built into the roster: time for protocol study, documentation, and a mandatory end-of-month protocol test. Not just nice-to-have — it's structured into the schedule.

## Reception (Separate)

Two receptionists split early/late coverage. One covers Saturday phone forwarding. Goes live when the reception team is ready — doesn't need to launch with the nursing roster.

Deferred. Get the structure right first, then agree the compensation model separately.
