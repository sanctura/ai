1. **A CIPN (peripheral neuropathy prevention) device** - cools patients' hands and feet during chemotherapy to prevent nerve damage
2. **A scalp cooling device** - prevents hair loss during chemotherapy

The total cost for 6 months is **£3,960 including VAT** (£3,300 ex VAT). The lease runs from 16 February to 15 August 2026.

## Why This Matters for Patients

Two of the most distressing side-effects of chemotherapy are peripheral neuropathy (numbness, tingling and pain in the hands and feet) and hair loss. These side-effects significantly impact quality of life and can lead to dose reductions or patients discontinuing treatment altogether.

Hilotherapy is a medical-grade, processor-controlled cooling system that maintains a constant temperature during chemotherapy infusion. Unlike consumer frozen gloves, it provides reproducible, degree-accurate temperature control with proper clinical governance.

## The Clinical Evidence

This is not experimental. Hilotherm is backed by published peer-reviewed evidence including a randomised controlled trial:

- **RCT in oxaliplatin patients** (Coolbrandt et al., ESMO Open 2023): 100% of cooled patients were free of significant neuropathy at 12 weeks vs 80.5% in the control group (p=0.006). Acute neuropathy symptoms were reduced by over 90%.
- **Comparative trial in breast cancer** (Coolbrandt et al., Breast Cancer Research & Treatment 2022): Significant neuropathy reduced from 61.3% to 43.6% compared with frozen gloves (p=0.013), with significantly better patient comfort.
- **108-patient real-world study** (Kostara & Schaper, DGS 2023): 96.3% of patients avoided clinically significant neuropathy. Zero nail toxicity across all patients.
- **UK centres already using it**: GenesisCare UK and The Royal Marsden NHS Foundation Trust have both published positive evaluation data.

## The Financial Case

**Cost**: £3,960 (inc VAT) for 6 months - this is the total exposure.

**Revenue**: We propose modest patient fees of £75-£195 per infusion session. Under conservative assumptions of 6-8 patients per month, the service covers its own costs during the pilot period.

**Path to purchase**: If we proceed to buy the equipment after the pilot, the £3,300 lease payment is credited against the purchase price. No sunk cost.

**Synergistic benefit**: Patients actively choose where to receive chemotherapy based on available supportive care. This service will attract and retain patients for chemotherapy at Sanctura, generating revenue beyond the cooling fees themselves.
