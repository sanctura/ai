**Introduction of Medical-Grade Limb Cryotherapy (CIPN) and Scalp Cooling at Sanctura**

**Executive Summary**  
Approval is requested for a 6-month lease pilot of one Hilotherm CIPN system and one Hilotherm scalp cooling (CIA) unit. This will position Sanctura as a fully integrated supportive oncology centre, increase chemotherapy uptake, and introduce a sustainable revenue stream while protecting patients from two of the most distressing toxicities of treatment: neuropathy and alopecia.

**Clinical Rationale**  
Chemotherapy-induced peripheral neuropathy (CIPN) remains one of the most functionally significant toxicities associated with neurotoxic chemotherapy regimens. Prospective data (Coolbrandt et al.) demonstrate a meaningful reduction in Grade ≥2 neuropathy with controlled limb cryotherapy. Unlike consumer product frozen gloves (i.e SuziePads), medical-grade systems provide reproducible temperature regulation, improved tolerability, and appropriate governance oversight.

Alopecia continues to influence patients’ decisions about where to receive chemotherapy. Lloyd’s clinical currently offers home-based Paxman systems, which are bulky and logistically complex. Sanctura can provide in-clinic scalp cooling within the infusion pathway, offering a seamless one-stop service.

**Strategic Positioning**  
This initiative places Sanctura among the few UK centres offering fully integrated chemotherapy delivery with medical-grade CIPN prevention and scalp cooling in the same setting. It aligns with early adoption trends seen across major private providers including GenesisCare, Ramsay Health Care UK and Spire Healthcare, as well as selected NHS oncology centres supported through charitable funding.

The model differentiates Sanctura from Lloyd’s by offering supervised, in-chair supportive cooling rather than a fragmented home-lease pathway. It strengthens Sanctura’s positioning as a forward-thinking precision oncology provider capable of generating publishable outcome data. **Prof. Stebbing** has confirmed he would recommend this intervention to appropriate patients treated at Sanctura.

**Proposed patient Fee Structure**  
To ensure adoption, accessibility and sustainability, a modest fee model is proposed.

* CIPN cryotherapy: £75–£100 per infusion  
* Scalp cooling: £120–£150 per infusion  
* Integrated package (CIPN \+ scalp cooling): £175–£195 per infusion 

Under conservative utilisation assumptions (approximately 6–8 patients per month across all options), the lease cost is covered and the service becomes self-sustaining during the pilot phase. Transition to capital purchase further improves long-term margin.

**Implementation Plan**  
Phase 1: 6-month lease pilot with optional self-funded patient add-on. Clinical impact will be tracked using CTCAE neuropathy grading and patient-reported outcomes alongside financial monitoring.  
Phase 2: Review uptake, outcomes and financial performance; if justified, proceed to capital acquisition with lease payments credited toward purchase.
