# Executive Summary

## Initiative: Medical-Grade Limb Cryotherapy (CIPN) & Scalp Cooling at Sanctura

**Proposed by:** Maria Vega, Nurse, Sanctura
**Endorsed by:** Prof. Justin Stebbing (Referring Oncologist)
**Date:** February 2026
**Status:** Board approval requested

### Overview

Sanctura is seeking board approval for a 6-month lease pilot of one Hilotherm CIPN system and one Hilotherm scalp cooling (CIA) unit. The total cost is **£3,960 inc VAT** for the 6-month period.

This initiative will:
- Protect cancer patients from two of the most distressing chemotherapy side-effects: **peripheral neuropathy** (nerve damage in hands/feet) and **alopecia** (hair loss)
- Position Sanctura as a **fully integrated supportive oncology centre** - one of very few UK centres offering medical-grade CIPN prevention and scalp cooling alongside chemotherapy
- Generate a **self-sustaining revenue stream** through modest patient fees (£75-£195 per infusion session)
- Attract additional patients to Sanctura for chemotherapy treatment (synergistic effect on core revenue)
- Enable **publishable outcome data** in collaboration with Prof. Stebbing, enhancing Sanctura's academic credentials
- Differentiate from competitors (e.g. Lloyd's) who only offer bulky home-based Paxman systems

### The Ask

Approval for a 6-month lease pilot at £3,960 (inc VAT), with the option to transition to capital purchase where lease payments are credited toward the purchase price, providing a stronger long-term business case.

### Clinical Evidence

The Hilotherm system is backed by multiple peer-reviewed studies and real-world evaluations from major UK and European centres including The Royal Marsden, GenesisCare, and University Hospitals Leuven. Evidence demonstrates:
- 93-96% of patients avoid clinically significant (Grade 2+) neuropathy
- Superior performance vs frozen gloves (the traditional approach)
- Significantly better patient comfort and compliance
- Statistically significant results in RCTs for both taxane and oxaliplatin regimens
