# Clinical Rationale

## The Problem: Chemotherapy Side-Effects

### Chemotherapy-Induced Peripheral Neuropathy (CIPN)
- One of the most functionally significant toxicities of neurotoxic chemotherapy
- Affects up to **42% of patients** treated with paclitaxel; severe (Grade 2+) neuropathy in **27%** receiving weekly paclitaxel
- Symptoms: tingling, numbness, pain in hands and feet - can be chronic and persist for years
- Impacts physical, social, and emotional wellbeing and quality of life
- Leads to **dose reductions or treatment discontinuation**, affecting long-term prognosis
- No effective pharmacological prevention or treatment currently exists (ASCO Guidelines)

### Chemotherapy-Induced Alopecia (CIA)
- Continues to influence patients' decisions about **where to receive chemotherapy**
- Significant psychological impact on patients
- Current competitor offering (Lloyd's): bulky, logistically complex home-based Paxman systems
- Sanctura can offer in-clinic scalp cooling within the infusion pathway - a seamless one-stop service

### Nail Toxicity
- Occurs in up to **40% of patients** treated with taxanes
- Paronychia and onycholysis can drastically affect daily functioning and quality of life
- Hilotherapy also provides protection against nail toxicity

## The Solution: Hilotherapy (Controlled Medical-Grade Cooling)

### How It Works
- Processor-controlled cooling method accurate to the degree
- Uses an electrically driven closed cooling system with demineralised water
- Provides continuous, constant-temperature cooling of hands, feet, and/or scalp
- Temperature setting: 10-17°C depending on generation and protocol
- Standard protocol: 30 min pre-cooling, during chemotherapy infusion, 30 min post-cooling

### Why Hilotherapy vs Frozen Gloves
| Feature | Hilotherapy | Frozen Gloves |
|---------|------------|---------------|
| Temperature control | Constant, degree-accurate | Rapidly rising, unstable |
| Patient comfort | Significantly better (p<0.0001) | High discomfort, >50% dropout in some studies |
| Efficacy (Grade 2+ side-effects) | 43.6% incidence | 61.3% incidence (p=0.013) |
| Staff burden | Minimal - set and monitor | Must change gloves every 45 min |
| Availability | Currently available | Becoming unavailable in some markets |
| Fidelity | No intentional fidelity issues | 24.2% had fidelity issues |

### Applicable Chemotherapy Regimens
- **Taxane-based**: Paclitaxel (weekly or 3-weekly), Docetaxel
- **Platinum-based**: Oxaliplatin (FOLFOX, CAPOX, FOLFIRINOX)
- **Cancer types**: Breast, colorectal, GI, gynaecological, pancreatic, oesophageal

## Guideline Endorsements
- **ESMO Clinical Practice Guidelines**: Frozen gloves/socks should be systematically advised in patients treated with taxanes [II, A]; prevention of CIPN with cryotherapy can be considered [II, C]
- **ASCO Guideline**: Available data support that cryotherapy may prevent CIPN symptoms and appears reasonably safe
- **ESMO-EONS-EANO Guidelines**: Prevention of CIPN with cryotherapy can be considered [II, C]
