# Evidence Base

## Key Studies Supporting Hilotherapy

### 1. Coolbrandt et al. (2022) - Hilotherapy vs Frozen Gloves in Breast Cancer
**"Preventing taxane-related peripheral neuropathy, pain and nail toxicity"**
*Breast Cancer Research and Treatment* | Prospective self-controlled trial | NCT04659980

- **Setting**: University Hospitals Leuven, Belgium (June 2019 - November 2020)
- **Patients**: 62 early breast cancer patients; weekly paclitaxel 80mg/m2 or 3-weekly docetaxel 75mg/m2
- **Design**: Self-controlled - hilotherapy on right hand/foot vs frozen gloves on left
- **Key Results**:
  - Grade 2+ side-effects: **43.6% hilotherapy vs 61.3% frozen gloves (p=0.013)**
  - Any-grade side-effects: 85.5% vs 90.3% (p=1.000 - similar)
  - Grade 2+ neuropathy: 40.3% vs 50.0% (p=0.109)
  - Grade 2+ pain: **24.2% vs 37.1% (p=0.039)**
  - Nail toxicities: **59.7% vs 72.6% (p=0.039)**
  - Patient comfort: **significantly better for hilotherapy across all measures (p<0.0001)**
  - Time-to-event: all hazard ratios favour hilotherapy (21-39% risk reductions)
  - No intentional fidelity issues with hilotherapy vs 24.2% with frozen gloves
- **Conclusion**: Hilotherapy superior to frozen gloves for preventing Grade 2+ side-effects; significantly more comfortable

### 2. Coolbrandt et al. (2023) - RCT of Hilotherapy in Oxaliplatin
**"A randomized controlled trial of hand/foot-cooling by hilotherapy to prevent oxaliplatin-related peripheral neuropathy"**
*ESMO Open* | Phase II RCT | NCT04913376

- **Setting**: University Hospitals Leuven, Belgium (May 2021 - January 2022)
- **Patients**: 77 patients with digestive system malignancies (colorectal 62.3%, oesophageal 18.2%, pancreatic 15.6%); oxaliplatin-based chemotherapy
- **Design**: RCT, 1:1 randomisation, hilotherapy at 11°C vs standard care (no cooling)
- **Key Results**:
  - **Primary endpoint**: Grade 2 neuropathy-free rate at 12 weeks: **100% hilotherapy vs 80.5% control (p=0.006)**
  - At 24 weeks: **66.0% vs 49.2% (p=0.039)**
  - Acute numbness/tingling: OR 0.05 (p<0.0001) - massive reduction
  - Acute pain: OR 0.06 (p<0.0001)
  - Cold sensitivity: OR 0.02 (p<0.0001)
  - Pharyngeal cold sensitivity: OR 0.14 (p=0.0005)
  - Duration of acute neuropathy: **3.12 fewer days (p=0.0002)**
  - Treatment alterations-free rate at 12 weeks: 93.5% vs 83.4% (p=0.131)
  - Comfort: majority rated neutral to very comfortable
- **Conclusion**: First RCT on cooling during oxaliplatin; statistically significant prophylactic effect on Grade 2 OIPN; well tolerated

### 3. GenesisCare UK - Real-World Evaluation (UKONS Poster)
**"CIPN and the Use of Hilotherm for Prevention"**
*Anna McNally, Principal SACT Nurse; Carys Jenkins, Service Development Manager*

- **Setting**: GenesisCare UK
- **Patients**: 44 breast cancer patients receiving taxane chemotherapy
- **Key Results**:
  - Grade 0 (no symptoms): **63.6% Hilotherm vs 9.5% historical control (Schaper 2023)**
  - Grade 2-3: **6.8% Hilotherm vs 45.8% control**
  - Patient experience: 47.4% "Very good", 47.4% "Good", 5.3% "Average"
- **Limitations**: No local control group, some incomplete data
- **Conclusion**: Hilotherm significantly reduced CIPN incidence and severity; overwhelmingly positive patient feedback

### 4. Royal Marsden NHS Foundation Trust - Service Evaluation (UKONS Poster, August 2025)
**"Evaluating controlled cooling therapy for the prevention of CIPN"**
*Laura Collantes-Espinosa et al.*

- **Setting**: The Royal Marsden NHS Foundation Trust, London
- **Patients**: 12 patients (5 GI, 4 breast, 3 gynaecological); oxaliplatin, carboplatin/paclitaxel, paclitaxel regimens
- **Key Results**:
  - Of 8 patients adhering to device: 4 had no CIPN, 4 developed Grade 1 only
  - At 3 months post-treatment: 7 had no CIPN, 1 had Grade 1
  - Treatment modifications due to CIPN: only 1 patient
  - Adherence: 8/12 (67%) - dropouts mainly due to time wearing gloves
- **Conclusion**: Controlled cooling feasible and safe; most patients developed only mild CIPN; supports further investigation

### 5. Kostara & Schaper (DGS 2023) - 2nd Generation Hilotherapy
**"The HILOTHERAPY - Avoids chemotherapy-induced polyneuropathy (CIPN) and nail toxicity"**
*GynOnco Düsseldorf / Luisenkrankenhaus Düsseldorf*

- **Patients**: 108 breast cancer patients receiving taxane-containing chemotherapy
- **Device**: 2nd generation Hilotherapy with pressure cooling cuffs (15-17°C)
- **Key Results**:
  - CIPN during treatment: Grade 0: 52.8%, Grade 1: 38.0%, Grade 2: 9.2%, Grade 3: 0%
  - At last therapy: Grade 0: 66.7%, Grade 1: 29.6%, Grade 2: 3.7%, Grade 3: 0%
  - **96.3% avoided Grade 2+ CIPN at last therapy**
  - **Zero nail toxicity across all 108 patients**
  - Comparison with 1st gen: slight improvement (Grade 2 at last therapy: 3.7% vs 6.6%)
  - Post-cooling comparison: 97.4% (with post-cooling) vs 95.7% (without) avoided Grade 2+ - negligible difference
- **Conclusion**: 93-96% of patients avoid clinically significant CIPN; Grade 3 no longer occurs with 2nd gen; post-cooling may be dispensed with

## Summary of Evidence Strength

| Endpoint | Evidence Level | Key Finding |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| CIPN prevention (taxane) | Prospective comparative trial | 43.6% vs 61.3% Grade 2+ (p=0.013) |
| CIPN prevention (oxaliplatin) | Phase II RCT | 100% vs 80.5% Grade 2-free at 12wk (p=0.006) |
| Patient comfort | Multiple studies | Consistently significantly better than frozen gloves |
| Acute neuropathy (oxaliplatin) | RCT | Massive reductions (OR 0.02-0.14, all p<0.001) |
| Nail toxicity prevention | Multiple studies | Zero nail toxicity in 108-patient study |
| Real-world UK adoption | GenesisCare, Royal Marsden | Positive outcomes, high patient satisfaction |
