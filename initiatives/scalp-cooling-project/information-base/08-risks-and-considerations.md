# Risks & Considerations

## Risks

### 1. Patient Uptake Risk (Low)
- **Risk**: Insufficient patients opt for the service
- **Mitigation**: Conservative model only requires 6-8 patients/month; Prof. Stebbing has committed to referrals; growing awareness of CIPN prevention
- **Downside**: Limited to £3,960 total exposure on a 6-month lease

### 2. Clinical Contraindications
- **Not suitable for**: Patients with Raynaud's phenomenon, cold intolerance/allergy, peripheral vascular disease, nail disease, existing Grade 2+ neuropathy
- **Mitigation**: Proper patient screening; aligns with standard oncology assessment

### 3. Patient Compliance/Adherence
- **Risk**: Some patients may not tolerate the cooling
- **Evidence**: Adherence rates are significantly better with hilotherapy vs frozen gloves (0% intentional non-compliance vs 24.2% with frozen gloves in Coolbrandt 2022)
- **Note**: Royal Marsden found 8/12 (67%) adherence; dropouts mainly due to time wearing gloves on feet
- **Mitigation**: Patient education, option to cool hands only if feet are not tolerated

### 4. Staff Training
- **Risk**: Staff need to be trained on device operation
- **Mitigation**: Device is straightforward to operate; Maria Vega is already the clinical champion; Hilotherm likely provides training support

### 5. Limitation of Mobility During Treatment
- **Risk**: Some patients find mobility restriction burdensome during cooling
- **Evidence**: Kostara study - mobility limitation rated as most burdensome aspect (~25% of sessions rated as burdensome)
- **Mitigation**: Patient counselling on expectations; hands-only option for less tolerant patients

## Considerations

### Scalp Cooling Limitations
- Best results when scalp temperature below 18°C
- Effectiveness varies by chemotherapy regimen
- Most evidence is for taxane and anthracycline regimens
- Patient hair type/thickness may affect outcomes

### Evidence Gaps
- Most robust evidence is for taxane-based and oxaliplatin-based regimens
- Self-controlled studies have inherent detection bias (unblinded)
- Long-term follow-up data (beyond 24 weeks) is limited
- ASCO guidelines note that proof of benefit has not yet been fully established for CIPN prevention (though trending positively)

### Governance
- Medical-grade device - appropriate clinical governance required
- Unlike consumer products (e.g. SuziePads), provides reproducible temperature regulation
- Patient consent and information processes needed
- Outcome tracking should follow CTCAE criteria
