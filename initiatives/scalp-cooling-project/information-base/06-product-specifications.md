- Provides continuous flow therapy for constant temperature cooling
- Unlike frozen gloves: no temperature fluctuation, no need to change gloves mid-session

## Clinical Protocol

### CIPN (Hands & Feet)
- **Temperature**: 10-17°C (varies by generation; 1st gen: 10-12°C, 2nd gen: 15-17°C)
- **Pre-cooling**: 30 minutes before chemotherapy infusion
- **During**: Throughout chemotherapy infusion
- **Post-cooling**: 30 minutes after infusion ends (recent evidence suggests this may be optional)
- **Total cooling time**: ~2-3 hours depending on infusion length

### CIA (Scalp Cooling)
- Applied before, during, and after chemotherapy infusion
- Optimal scalp temperature: below 18°C for best results
- In-clinic, supervised application within infusion pathway

## Generations of Cooling Cuffs

### 1st Generation (Gloves/Booties)
- Temperature: 10-12°C
- Results at last therapy: Grade 0: 52.3%, Grade 1: 40.4%, Grade 2: 6.6%, Grade 3: 0.6%

### 2nd Generation (Pressure Cooling Cuffs)
- Temperature: 15-17°C (higher but with improved pressure = better skin contact)
- Lowers skin surface to ~17-18°C
- Results at last therapy: Grade 0: 66.7%, Grade 1: 29.6%, Grade 2: 3.7%, Grade 3: 0%
- Slight improvement over 1st generation; eliminates Grade 3 entirely
